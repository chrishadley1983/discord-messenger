### Coffee Basque recipe ⬇️

**City/Region:** General | **Category:** Food And Drink | **Cost Level:** mid | **Source:** @mattyedgell | **Likes:** 10,829

**Caption:**
> Coffee Basque recipe ⬇️

Another day, another delicious basque! This time it’s got the crunch of a buttery @biscoff__uk biscuit base followed by the delicious velvety centre, rich with coffee 🤤 

If you had to have one of these for your Christmas Day dessert, what flavour would you go for? I can’t decide 😂

Coffee Basque
Ingredients
100g milk chocolate 
150g soured cream
250g double cream
880g cream cheese
225g golden caster sugar
4 large eggs
35g plain flour
1/2tsp flaked sea salt
2tsp espresso powder
1tbsp crushed coffee beans (optional)
200g biscoff biscuits
70g butter (melted)

Chocolate Sauce
I cheated and used @waitrose chocolate sauce and it worked perfectly!

Method
1.Pre-heat oven to 220C and line the bottom and sides of an 8inch spring form tin with greaseproof paper. Blitz the biscuits into a fine crumb and then combine with the butter. Spread evenly across the base, pushing down with the back of a spoon and then set aside. 

2.Infuse the cream by placing the cream and coffee into a saucepan and heating until simmering. Remove from the heat and allow to sit for 30mins. Then make a ganache by reheating the cream until simmering. Strain over the chopped chocolate, allow to sit for 2mins and mix until combined.

3.Add the cream cheese into a large bowl and slowly beat until smooth. 

4.Individually add the eggs, beating well between each addition. Add the sugar and combine before adding the sour cream. Once combined, pour in the cooled ganache and slow incorporate fully.

5.Sift in the flour and salt then beat for 1-2 minutes to ensure there is a smooth mix.

6.Pour the mixture into the tin through a sift to remove any lumps and then tap on the work surface 3-5 times to remove any air bubbles.

7.Bake for 27-30 minutes and then remove from the oven and allow to cool. Remember to remove with the significant wobble to get the soft centre. Once cooled, place in the fridge overnight.

8.To serve, remove the Basque from the fridge 10-15mins before serving then us
