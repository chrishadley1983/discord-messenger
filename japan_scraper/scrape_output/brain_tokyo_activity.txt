# Japan 2026 — Tokyo Activitys (15 places)
Scraped from Reddit + YouTube, March 2026.

- **Big Echo**: Popular karaoke chain recommended for ending a Tokyo night out, with all-night availability.
- **Fukagawa-Edo Museum** [Koto]: A life-sized reproduction of Edo-era street scenes, highlighted as one of the top sights during a Tokyo visit.
- **Ghibli Museum** [Kichijoji]: A must-visit Studio Ghibli themed museum in Inokashira Park area; advance ticket booking required as it sells out.
- **Mori Art Museum** [Roppongi]: Part of the Roppongi Hills complex, recommended as a contemporary art museum with city views from Tokyo City View.
- **Mt. Takao** [Takaosanguchi]: Popular hiking destination accessible from Shinjuku, magical summit experience.
- **Ossan Rental**: Unique service where you can rent a retired older man to spend time with, go shopping, watch movies, or learn about Japanese culture.
- **Seikado Bunko Museum** [Setagaya]: Museum hosting wonderful teaware exhibits including National Treasure chawan.
- **Shibuya Sky** [Shibuya]: Preferred over Tokyo Skytree during the night for the stunning city light views; arrival 30 minutes before booked slot recommended.
- **Tokyo Disneyland** [Maihama]: Praised as way better than American counterparts - cleaner, more organised.
- **Tokyo Edo Open Air Architectural Museum**: Very chill place with interesting buildings from different eras; less crowded alternative to major sites.
- **Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building Observatory** [Shinjuku]: Recommended as a free observation deck for city views, avoiding the need for timed slots unlike Shibuya Sky or Skytree.
- **Tokyo National Museum** [Ueno]: Recommended as part of a comprehensive Ueno area day covering the museum district.
- **Yah Kart Asakusa** [Asakusa]: An electric street karting company in Asakusa offering a one-hour route; recommended as a tourist activity without annoying noise or smell.
- **teamLab Borderless** [Azabudai]: Strongly preferred over teamLab Planets for its variety of rooms, feeling of space despite crowds, and higher quality video projection experience.
- **teamLab Planets** [Toyosu]: An immersive digital art experience; recommended but considered second to Borderless for variety; the EN Tea House inside noted as a highlight.
