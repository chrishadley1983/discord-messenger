# Japan 2026 — Other Cities Recommendations
Scraped from Reddit + YouTube, March 2026.

## Hakone (8 places)
- **Hakone Open-Air Museum** (activity): Outdoor sculpture museum in Hakone featuring works by Picasso and others set against mountain scenery.
- **Pola Museum of Art** (activity) [Gora]: Art museum nestled inside Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park, offering exhibitions of Impressionist and contemporary art surrounded by nature.
- **Gora Park** (park) [Gora]: Highlighted as one of the top sights during a Hakone walking tour itinerary.
- **Onshi-Hakone Park** (park) [Hakone]: Recommended for a very early morning walk to view Mt Fuji before tourists arrive.
- **Itoh Dining by Nobu** (restaurant) [Gora] | Tabelog 3.56*: Rated S (highest) - amazing food at great prices, one of the best meals of the trip.
- **Gora Kadan** (ryokan) [Gora] | Tabelog 3.61*: Mentioned as a comparison ryokan option in Hakone noted for its onsen facilities, though with normal warm water rather than natural onsen water in rooms.
- **Hakone Ginyu** (ryokan): A ryokan with a private onsen room and breathtaking views, though reviewer found it past its prime with shabby decor in the actual rooms despite great common areas.
- **Hakone Shrine** (shrine) [Lake Ashi] | Tabelog 3.42*: Sacred Shinto shrine on the shores of Lake Ashi in Hakone, featuring a famous torii gate rising from the water.

## Nara (8 places)
- **Cafe NAGY** (cafe): Cafe near Hasedera recommended as a stop during a visit to the lesser-known parts of Nara Prefecture.
- **Sorairo Coffee** (cafe): Local cafe in Nara City recommended as a hidden gem for those exploring beyond the main tourist sights.
- **Sanshu-tei** (restaurant): Traditional restaurant in Nara City featured as a dining recommendation during exploration of the city's hidden gems.
- **Nakatanidou** (shop): Famous for dramatic live mochi-pounding demonstrations; sells the most famous yomogi (mugwort) mochi in Nara City for ¥200.
- **Hase-dera** (temple): Undervisited temple with a hallway-like roofed stairway, much quieter than Kyoto temples.
- **Hasedera Temple** (temple) [Hasedera]: Mountain temple in the Hasedera area of Nara Prefecture, famous for a spectacular hydrangea display in late June.
- **Hōzan-ji** (temple) [Ikoma]: Forest temple on Mount Ikoma, a lesser-known Nara gem accessible via a cat-shaped cable car, set in atmospheric woodland.
- **Todai-ji Nigatsu-do** (temple): Sub-temple of Todai-ji offering the best sunset view in Nara, overlooking the city and surrounding landscape.

## Fukuoka (6 places)
- **Kego Tenjiku** (bar): Hidden apartment bar run by a heavily tattooed chef serving delicious omelets, wings, and drinks in a unique intimate setting.
- **Ohori Park** (park): A must-visit park in Fukuoka City.
- **Yasube** (restaurant) [Nishinakasu, Chuo-ku]: Traditional oden shop run by an 83-year-old grandpa, cheap and affordable, a touching hidden gem.
- **Yatai Keiji** (restaurant): Fukuoka's most famous yatai (food stall), open until 2AM, so popular it is opening an international branch in Barcelona.
- **Dazaifu Tenmangu** (shrine) [Dazaifu]: Key shrine attraction for a day trip from Fukuoka.
- **Nanzoin Temple** (temple): Home to a large reclining Buddha statue; must-see in Fukuoka.

## Kanazawa (6 places)
- **Higashi Chaya District** (activity) [Higashi Chaya]: Beautifully preserved geisha district in Kanazawa with traditional tea houses, sake shops and gold leaf craft stores.
- **Omicho Market** (market): Kanazawa's main fresh seafood and produce market, recommended as an essential morning visit for food lovers.
- **Kenrokuen Garden** (park): One of Japan's three great gardens and a top Kanazawa attraction, praised for its seasonal beauty and landscaping.
- **Kourin Sushi** (restaurant): Good quality sushi at reasonable prices (1860 yen).
- **Omicho Ichibazushi** (restaurant) [Omicho Market]: Excellent kaitenzushi visited twice for remarkably fresh fish, especially the squid.
- **Oyama Shrine** (shrine): Unique shrine in Kanazawa featuring a Dutch-influenced gate, recommended as part of a two-day Kanazawa itinerary.

## Hiroshima (5 places)
- **Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park** (activity) [Naka Ward]: Recommended as a first stop in Hiroshima, with the atomic bomb dome specifically suggested to be seen at night.
- **Itsukushima Island** (activity) [Miyajima]: Recommended to take the first ferry of the morning to spend a whole day seeing the Great Torii at low and high tide, feeding deer, and seeing the shrines.
- **Grill Hikoboshi** (restaurant): Intimate no-menu grill where the chef crafted a personalised multi-course meal; best culinary experience of poster's life.
- **Oko Q** (restaurant) [Hatsukaichi]: A Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki restaurant with a very friendly owner serving delicious food, highly recommended.
- **Itsukushima Shrine** (shrine) [Miyajima]: World-famous floating torii gate shrine on Miyajima island, one of Japan's most photographed landmarks.

## Sendai (5 places)
- **Arahama Elementary School Tsunami Ruins** (activity) [Arahama]: Called a must-see historical site documenting the 2011 tsunami, listed as a hidden gem recommendation for Sendai.
- **Matsushima** (activity): Recommended for the scenic small islands, though the reviewer noted the view isn't as spectacular as Ha Long Bay.
- **Date no Gyutan Honpo** (restaurant): One of the top recommended gyutan (cow tongue) restaurants in Sendai.
- **Gyutan no Issen** (restaurant): One of the popular gyutan (cow tongue) restaurants recommended as a local specialty experience in Sendai.
- **Zenjiro Sendai Station Gyutan Dori** (restaurant) [Sendai Station]: A gyutan restaurant in the Gyutan Dori area of Sendai Station, recommended for the local specialty of cow tongue.

## Yamanashi (5 places)
- **Fujiyama Onsen** (onsen) [Fujiyoshida]: Described as incredible and one of the reviewer's favourite onsens in Japan, adjacent to Fuji-Q Highland.
- **Hotou Fudo** (restaurant) [Kawaguchiko]: Rated 8/10 for lunch near Lake Kawaguchi, serving hoto noodles, a Yamanashi specialty.
- **Fufu Kawaguchiko** (ryokan) [Kawaguchiko]: A ryokan near Lake Kawaguchi rated 9/10, with excellent dinner and breakfast kaiseki; pacing of dinner noted as very long.
- **Kozantei Ubuya** (ryokan) [Kawaguchiko]: A ryokan at Lake Kawaguchi used during a Fuji day, noted during a detailed 18-day trip report.
- **Nishiyama Onsen Keiunkan** (ryokan): The oldest inn in the world, offering private baths, private dining, and seasonal kaiseki meals.

## Kamakura (4 places)
- **Shirasu Cafe Kamakura Jinbei** (cafe) [Kamakura]: The go-to spot for shirasu (whitebait), a Kamakura delicacy of tiny translucent fish served over rice.
- **Verve Coffee Roasters** (cafe) [Kamakura]: Speciality coffee roaster in Kamakura recommended as an energy stop before the Daibutsu hiking trail.
- **Shintomi Ramen and Sushi** (restaurant) [Kita-Kamakura]: Simple and good ramen and sushi restaurant at the start of the Kamakura day trip hike route, near Kita-Kamakura Station.
- **Engaku-ji Temple** (temple) [Kita-Kamakura]: Major Zen Buddhist temple at Kita-Kamakura station, recommended as the first stop on a Kamakura day trip from Tokyo.

## Niigata (4 places)
- **The Last Classroom** (activity) [Tokamachi]: A one-of-a-kind art installation in an abandoned school turned art exhibit about isolation, described as unforgettable.
- **Ebiju DELUX** (restaurant): Recommended for ebi shoyu ramen — praised as not just another ramen, especially for shrimp flavor lovers.
- **Furukawa Sengyo** (restaurant) [Honchodori]: A fish store serving kaisendon and grilled kamasu with a friendly obachan who shows you what's fresh each day.
- **Marutomisuisan** (restaurant) [Niigata Station]: An izakaya near Niigata Station serving grilled nodoguro (blackthroat seaperch), a local Niigata specialty.

## Sapporo (4 places)
- **Mt. Moiwa** (activity): Rated 9/10 observation deck at 531m, accessed by tram and cable car; arrive before sunset.
- **Sapporo JR Tower** (activity): Rated 10/10 as an observation deck - the tallest building in Hokkaido with 360 degree views.
- **Hachikyo** (restaurant): Famous Japanese seafood restaurant known for massive salmon roe bowls where diners must pay a fine if they leave any rice — a mark of respect for fishermen.
- **Sumire** (restaurant): Cited multiple times as one of the best miso ramen in Japan, a landmark Sapporo ramen destination.

## Other (3 places)
- **Kasugatei** (restaurant): Known for abura soba (oil noodles), recommended for those wanting to experience a bold and filling Japanese dish.
- **Marugame Seimen** (restaurant): A chain offering cheap, fast and authentic udon, recommended as the go-to udon experience in Japan.
- **Mendokoro Koyomi** (restaurant): A ramen teishoku (set meal) restaurant praised by a local as the best post-work ramen experience.

## Himeji (3 places)
- **Himeji Castle** (activity): One of the original twelve surviving Japanese castles, stunning with cherry blossoms.
- **Koko-en Garden** (park): Gardens next to Himeji Castle that are almost empty compared to the castle.
- **Engyoji** (temple): Easily combined with Himeji Castle for a fantastic day trip with good views.

## Nagano (3 places)
- **Daio Wasabi Farm** (activity) [Azumino]: Wasabi farm visit in the Nagano area.
- **Jigokudani Monkey Park** (activity): Famous park where wild Japanese snow monkeys bathe in natural hot spring pools; a unique wildlife experience.
- **Snow Monkey Park** (activity) [Yudanaka]: Famous park where Japanese macaques bathe in hot springs.

## Takayama (3 places)
- **Hida Folk Village (Hida No Sato)** (activity): An open-air folk museum in Takayama recommended for a morning visit after the Miyagawa Morning Market.
- **Miyagawa Morning Market** (market): A traditional morning market in Takayama recommended as a top daily activity in the town.
- **Ryokan Asunaro** (ryokan): Praised as having some of the best kaiseki cuisine ever tasted, with brilliant facilities and a seasonal menu that changes with every visit; highly recommended for anyone traveling through rural Gifu.

## Matsumoto (2 places)
- **Matsumoto Castle** (activity): One of the original twelve surviving Japanese castles, praised for unique location and beauty.
- **Ramen Shop Tsubaki** (restaurant): Described as one of the best ramens had during a Japan trip, walking distance from Matsumoto Station.

## Uji (2 places)
- **Nakamura Tokichi** (cafe): Green tea dessert shop with next-level matcha desserts.
- **Tsuen Tea Shop** (cafe) | Tabelog 3.43*: Described as the oldest tea house in Japan and the world.

## Yamagata (2 places)
- **Hanikaida Guest House** (hotel) [Oguni-machi]: A log house guest house and recording studio built by owner Toshiharu deep in the Yamagata mountains, described as feeling more like visiting an old Japanese friend's mountain cabin than an Airbnb.
- **Ginzan Onsen** (onsen): Key overnight stop during a winter trip through Tohoku.

## Yamaguchi (2 places)
- **Akiyoshido Cave** (activity): Magnificently gorgeous cave with turquoise water visible through a crack; feels unreal.
- **Motonosumi Shrine** (shrine): Beautiful shrine with sea breeze, torii gates, and stunning duo-coloured sea view.

## Yokohama (2 places)
- **Kirin Beer Factory** (activity): Brewery tour offering a full factory tour with free tasting.
- **SHINYOKOHAMA RAUMEN MUSEUM** (activity) [Shin-Yokohama]: Ramen theme museum with restaurants recreating Japan's top regional ramen styles under one roof, recommended among 101 Tokyo-area activities.

## Asahikawa (1 places)
- **Aoba Ramen** (restaurant) | Tabelog 3.47*: Said to have lines out the door but 100% worth the wait for some of the best soy sauce ramen in Asahikawa.

## Awaji Island (1 places)
- **Awaji International Hotel The Sunplaza** (ryokan) [Sumoto]: Known for its spectacular 'Tamatebako' (Treasure Box) breakfast spread featuring local Awaji Island seasonal delicacies in a three-tier wooden box.

## Fujikawaguchiko (1 places)
- **Kozantei Ubuya** (ryokan) [Kawaguchiko]: Splurge ryokan with a private hot bath on the balcony overlooking stunning views.

## Fujisawa (1 places)
- **Enoshima-tei** (restaurant) [Enoshima]: A seafood restaurant in Enoshima serving shirasu (whitebait) set meals with three preparations: raw, boiled, and fried; the raw preparation was particularly recommended.

## Gunma (1 places)
- **Takaragawa Onsen** (onsen): Included as a memorable one-night stay during a 5-week cross-Japan trip covering multiple destinations.

## Hokkaido (1 places)
- **Matatabi Curry** (restaurant) [Bifuka]: Japan's most northern Japanese spice curry restaurant, described as absolutely delicious.

## Japan (1 places)
- **Yukichi Soba** (restaurant): Small shop run by a 74-year-old woman (obachan) that offers a 5.3kg ramen eating challenge with over 1.8kg of noodles to finish within a time limit.

## Kagoshima (1 places)
- **Kirishima National Park** (activity) [Kirishima]: Recommended for a long day hike during a two-week Kyushu road trip itinerary.

## Kurume (1 places)
- **Taiho Ramen** (restaurant): Described as the best ramen in Fukuoka/Kurume by the poster, a classic tonkotsu ramen establishment.

## Mie (1 places)
- **Akame 48 Waterfalls** (activity) [Nabari City]: A relatively easy hiking trail running alongside 48 waterfalls, 1.5-hour drive from Osaka, with a small salamander museum and ninja class for kids.

## Miyajima (1 places)
- **Kafuku Shokudo** (restaurant): Restaurant on Miyajima Island recommended for its oysters, a must-eat local specialty.

## Miyazaki (1 places)
- **Amanoiwato Shrine** (shrine) [Takachiho]: One of Japan's most significant shrines, the location of the famous Amaterasu sun goddess story.

## Nagoya (1 places)
- **Ramen Josui** (restaurant): Described as one of the best ramen restaurants in Nagoya.

## Naoshima (1 places)
- **Chichu Art Museum** (activity) [Naoshima]: Highly popular art museum that sells out same-day; pre-booking strongly recommended.

## Odawara (1 places)
- **Torimatsu** (restaurant) | Tabelog 3.37*: Highly recommended chicken-based ramen with exceptional broth, thin noodles, and Mars-coloured eggs.

## Oita (1 places)
- **Beppu Hells** (activity) [Beppu]: Called amazing overall though the crocodile hell was criticised for poor animal welfare conditions; the other hell pools highly enjoyed.

## Otsu (1 places)
- **Ishiyama-dera** (temple): Undervisited Kansai temple with strange rock structures and a National Treasure pagoda.

## Shizuoka (1 places)
- **Sawayaka** (restaurant): A hamburger steak restaurant wildly popular among Japanese locals with 2-hour queues, only available in Shizuoka Prefecture.

## Takamatsu (1 places)
- **Ritsurin Garden** (park): Amazing Japanese garden best visited in the morning when few visitors.

## Teshima (1 places)
- **Teshima Art Museum** (activity) [Teshima]: Popular art museum that sells out same-day; pre-booking strongly recommended.

## Toyota City (1 places)
- **Kanoya Confectionery** (shop) [Wakarimachi Station]: Traditional Japanese confectionery known for Wakaayu (young sweetfish-shaped wagashi) with a subtle soy sauce dough and chewy mochi-like texture.
