# Japan 2026 — Kyoto Recommendations (52 places)
Scraped from Reddit + YouTube, March 2026.

## Restaurants (15)
- **Gyukatsu Kyoto Katsugyu**: A gyukatsu restaurant where the sides, breading, and sauces were tasty, though the meat quality was critiqued as chewy.
- **Hachikian** [Marutamachi]: Known for exceptional oyakodon — described as the best the reviewer ever had — plus excellent udon; reservations required for the main store.
- **Honke Daiichi-Asahi**: Described as the #1 ramen shop in Kyoto City, 4 minutes from the train station.
- **Horitako**: Praised for its takoyaki in Kyoto.
- **Kikunoi Honten** [Higashiyama]: World-renowned 3-Michelin Star kaiseki restaurant over 100 years old, set in a mansion with tatami rooms, garden views, and seasonal menus prepared with 35 cooks.
- **Koisus**: A curry restaurant in Kyoto where staff said they use 20 spices, making an incredible curry that the visitor cannot stop thinking about.
- **Kyoto Engine Ramen**: Praised as one of the best ramen bowls the reviewer ever had during a visit to Kyoto.
- **Menya Inoichi**: Ramen spot where the poster waited four hours and declared it worth every minute.
- **Owariya** | Tabelog 3.36*: Japan's oldest soba noodle restaurant, established in 1465.
- **Premium Steak Wagyu Hanasato** [Gion]: Wagyu steakhouse where A5 grade steak was called the best of their lives, around 150 euros.
- **Ramen Sen-no-Kaze**: A ramen restaurant in Kyoto that the poster was still dreaming about.
- **Saryo Tesshin**: Highly recommended soba noodle restaurant in Kyoto, featured among 12 legendary Japanese food experiences.
- **Suzume** [Pontocho]: An izakaya in Pontocho's side alleys where chef/owner Aya speaks fluent English and creates an intimate dinner-party atmosphere with her small seasonal menu.
- **Yoshinosushi** [Pontocho]: Tucked in an alley off Pontocho, praised for some of the best sushi and sashimi ever tasted with kind English-speaking staff.
- **Zirael Vegan Restaurant** [Gion]: A vegan restaurant in Kyoto serving delicious and healthy vegan platters with rice and fresh salad, plus vegan yuzu beer.

## Cafes (9)
- **Cafe & Gallery Saika** [Miyama]: Small cafe in Miyama's thatched village of Kayabuki no Sato, recommended as a charming stop during a day trip.
- **Maccha House**: Matcha dessert cafe; the tiramisu scored 10/10.
- **Nakamura Tokichi Honten** [Uji]: Famous matcha dessert shop in Uji, Japan's matcha city, known for premium green tea sweets and parfaits.
- **Patisserie S**: Their Masala cake slice was described as one of the best cakes the reviewer had ever had.
- **SCHOOL BUS COFFEE STOP KYOTO**: Kyoto coffee shop recommended as a stop while spending a day exploring the city.
- **THE CITY BAKERY**: Bakery cafe in Kyoto mentioned as a place to visit during a day exploring the city.
- **Tsujiri Uji Honten** [Uji]: Flagship matcha shop in Uji recommended for lovers of green tea desserts and sweets.
- **WEEKENDERS COFFEE TOMINOKOJI** [Tominokoji]: Well-regarded Kyoto coffee shop recommended as part of a day exploring the city and day-trip areas.
- **Weekenders Coffee** [Gion]: Specialty coffee shop in the Gion area recommended for coffee-focused travellers.

## Markets (2)
- **Kyoto Tower Sando** [Kyoto Station]: Hidden food hall directly below Kyoto Tower and connected to Kyoto Station via underground passage, offering traditional dishes and Kyoto specialities with surprisingly short queues.
- **Nishiki Market** [Nishiki]: Known as the 'Kitchen of Kyoto', this covered shopping street is packed with street food, local produce, and traditional Japanese ingredients.

## Temples (10)
- **Byodo-in Temple** [Uji]: UNESCO World Heritage 11th-century temple in Uji with a famous phoenix hall reflected in a tranquil pond.
- **Eikando** [Okazaki]: Described as worth the entrance fee during an autumn foliage Kyoto walk along the Philosopher's Path.
- **Ginkaku-ji** [Higashiyama]: Beautiful temple at the northern end of the Philosopher's Path, though noted to be packed even late in the afternoon.
- **Jakko-in** [Ohara]: A smaller temple in the Ohara village area of Kyoto, recommended as part of an Ohara day trip.
- **Kiyomizu-dera** [Higashiyama]: Recommended to visit at 6am opening to experience the wooden stage and views over the city without crowds.
- **Kiyomizudera Temple** [Higashiyama]: Famous hilltop wooden stage temple in Kyoto's Higashiyama district, one of Japan's most iconic sights.
- **Kodai-ji** [Higashiyama]: Had almost nobody there in the afternoon, providing a nice break from the crowds of Kiyomizu-dera.
- **Nanzen-ji** [Okazaki]: The aqueduct at Nanzen-ji was described as surreal, visited as part of a full Higashiyama-to-Philosopher's Path walking day.
- **Sanzen-in** [Ohara]: A temple in the Ohara area of Kyoto, noted as busier than nearby alternatives but worth a visit.
- **Shoren-in** [Higashiyama]: Called the number one temple in Kyoto to visit, with a large garden and surprisingly big temple that was nearly empty at noon.

## Shrines (4)
- **Fushimi Inari** [Fushimi]: Strongly recommended to visit at 6am to experience the lower torii gates without crowds, which become wall-to-wall tourists by 9am.
- **Fushimi Inari Shrine** [Fushimi]: Iconic shrine famous for thousands of torii gates winding up a forested mountain; a must-visit Kyoto attraction.
- **Uji-jinja Shrine** [Uji]: Ancient shrine in Japan's famous matcha city of Uji, recommended as part of a day trip from Kyoto.
- **Yasaka Shrine** [Higashiyama]: Located near Shoren-in temple and Maruyama Park, recommended as part of the Higashiyama walking route.

## Parks (3)
- **Arashiyama Bamboo Forest** [Arashiyama]: Iconic bamboo grove in Arashiyama, one of Kyoto's most photographed and serene natural landmarks.
- **Maruyama Park** [Higashiyama]: Recommended alongside Yasaka Shrine and Shoren-in as part of a Higashiyama temple and park circuit.
- **Philosopher's Path** [Higashiyama]: Scenic canal-side walking path beautiful in autumn foliage.

## Activitys (4)
- **Miyako Odori** [Gion]: A geisha and maiko dance performance described as incredible; English audio headsets available; advance tickets recommended and punctuality essential.
- **Miyama Folk Museum** [Miyama]: Rural folk museum in Miyama's historic thatched-roof village, offering a glimpse into traditional Japanese rural life.
- **Nijo Castle**: UNESCO site where you walk through original shogun-era rooms; offers limited-entry Honmaru-goten Palace.
- **Suntory The Premium Malts Factory**: Guided factory tour with free beer tasting at the end.

## Hotels (3)
- **Hotel The Mitsui Kyoto**: Luxury hotel booked with points and cash, with Marriott Gold room upgrade.
- **Ritz Kyoto**: Described as one of the best hotels ever, with an excellent free breakfast buffet.
- **Sora Niwa Terrace** [Gion]: Right across from Gion with an awesome rooftop terrace and on-site onsen.

## Shops (2)
- **Mochi mochi**: Street food spot in Kyoto where you can watch traditional mochi being made by hand.
- **ShinPuhKan**: Shopping and dining complex in Kyoto recommended as a place to explore during a day in the city.
