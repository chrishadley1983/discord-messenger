# Japan 2026 — Tokyo Cafes (21 places)
Scraped from Reddit + YouTube, March 2026.

- **Alley Cafe** [Kichijoji]: A moody, comfortable cafe near Kichijoji station where you can chill for hours with great music.
- **Asakusa Chaya Tabanenoshi** [Asakusa]: Traditional-style tea house in Asakusa featured as a must-visit spot during a food tour of the area.
- **Asakusa Soratsuki** [Asakusa]: One of several food spots in Asakusa featured in a food tour of the historic district.
- **Cafe De L'Ambre** [Ginza]: Famous old-school kissaten coffee shop with a well-balanced house blend and aged beans.
- **Cafe Reissue**: Recommended for casual coffee drinkers, described as very fun.
- **Chacha Futatsume** [Asakusa]: Matcha-focused cafe in Asakusa recommended as part of a street food tour of the area.
- **Fuglen**: Two good locations in Tokyo praised for good vibes.
- **Ginza West Aoyama Garden** [Aoyama]: Featured as a spot for perfect Japanese souffle pancakes.
- **Glitch Coffee**: Specialty coffee shop recommended by locals.
- **Iyoshi Cola Shibuya Jingumae** [Jingumae]: The original craft cola manufacturer from Harajuku, using traditional Chinese medicine methods.
- **Jack in the Donut** [Shibuya]: Donut shop in Shibuya featured as one of the local food spots tourists typically miss.
- **Komeda Coffee Ten**: Beloved Japanese coffee chain known for its morning set and generous sandwiches, featured in a Tokyo activity guide.
- **MATCHA PASSPORT**: Matcha-focused cafe in Tokyo, featured among 101 recommended things to do in the city.
- **Mr. Donut**: Japan's most famous donut chain (known locally as 'Misdo'), celebrated for their mochi-textured pomme de ring donuts made with tapioca flour.
- **Nakajima Tea House** [Hamarikyu]: A traditional tea house inside Hamarikyu Gardens, listed as a recommended stop during a Tsukiji and Hamarikyu day.
- **Parfaiteria Bel** [Shibuya]: Parfait specialist with intricate monthly-changing parfaits using seasonal fruits.
- **Sarutahiko**: A decent coffee chain found throughout Tokyo.
- **Starbucks Reserve Roastery**: One of the biggest Starbucks in the world, designed like Willy Wonka's chocolate factory for coffee, with unique menu items like espresso tonic and cinema cappuccino.
- **Suzukien Asakusa** [Asakusa]: Recommended for the strongest matcha in Tokyo.
- **TRUFFLE & BREAD** [Miyoshi, Koto City]: Truffle-focused sandwich and bread shop in Koto City, featured as one of 18 best budget dining experiences in Tokyo.
- **Wired Bonbon** [Shinjuku]: A cafe in Shinjuku visited for lunch during a vegan/gluten-free trip to Japan, offering vegan options.
