# Japan 2026 — Osaka Recommendations (50 places)
Scraped from Reddit + YouTube, March 2026.

## Restaurants (24)
- **Honke Shibato**: Institution open for over 300 years and run by its 15th generation, serving some of the best unagi (eel) in Japan.
- **HozenJi Sushi Sakata**: Praised as the best sushi the reviewer had ever had, visited on Christmas night in Osaka.
- **Kadoya Shokudo**: One of the highest-rated ramen restaurants in Osaka, especially praised for its tsukemen (dipping ramen).
- **Kamojin**: Specialty duck yakitori restaurant; the duck skin skewer is particularly recommended.
- **Kani Doraku** [Dotonbori]: A crab restaurant in Dotonbori with advance reservations recommended, visited as a planned dinner stop.
- **Kawachiya** [Shinsekai]: Kushikatsu restaurant in the Shinsekai district, recommended as a must-eat during a visit to the area.
- **Kurogin Tuna Store** [Kuromon]: A tuna stand inside Kuromon Market where you pick your piece from a cooler and a chef makes it into sashimi/sushi in front of you.
- **Maruyoshi Sushi** [morning market]: Located at Osaka's morning market, serves the freshest mixed kaisen don (assorted seafood over rice) imaginable.
- **Matsuya (standing udon)** [Dotonbori]: Standing noodle bar next to Nonkiya that serves Osaka-style udon noodles with sweet bean curd for just 250 yen per bowl.
- **Mugen Ramen** [Namba]: Described as probably the best ramen ever had, recommended to order extra rich.
- **Mugito Mensuke**: Highly recommended for a single bowl of Osaka ramen, especially the special shoyu ramen.
- **Namba Okonomiyaki Ajinoya Honten** [Namba]: Original family-run okonomiyaki shop operating since 1965, now in its 4th generation; a signature Osaka dining institution.
- **Negiyaki Yamamoto**: Well-known okonomiyaki restaurant in Osaka, featured as one of 12 legendary Japanese food experiences.
- **OSAKA Kiichi-seiniku**: Yakiniku restaurant known for wagyu steak that stretches like an accordion.
- **Okonomiyaki Sanpei** [Dotonbori]: Okonomiyaki restaurant near Dotonbori tried on arrival night.
- **Shanghai cuisine RONTAN**: Recommended for grilled xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) with an extraordinarily juicy filling.
- **Shinsaibashi PARCO** [Shinsaibashi]: Underground dining area with 25 bars and eateries covering Spanish food, seafood izakayas, ramen, sushi, yakitori.
- **Stamina Ichiban** [Ikuno-ku]: Popular okonomiyaki and teppanyaki spot run by a skilled chef whose food sells out within minutes of opening.
- **Tengu** [Dotonbori]: A quintessential Osaka kushikatsu spot serving breaded and deep-fried skewers of assorted ingredients, best enjoyed with beer.
- **Ton (no-menu ramen)** [Ju Higashi]: Ramen shop with no menu that has been serving the same 900-yen tonkotsu ramen for over 40 years — you just sit down and get served.
- **Uncle Rikuro's** [Namba]: Famous for its jiggly cheesecake in Namba, Osaka.
- **Yakiniku Like** [Shinsaibashi]: Japanese BBQ chain visited on first evening in Osaka as a convenient solo-dining option.
- **Yakitori Goichi** [Higobashi]: Second highest-rated yakitori in Osaka on Tabelog (4.24), standout skewers using tajima-dori chicken.
- **YumeTori**: A yakitori restaurant that sparked the poster's obsession with yakitori.

## Cafes (4)
- **Amanojaku**: Known for chewy crepe batter topped with custard brûlée in an irresistible combination.
- **Dekasan**: Famous for a giant tamago sando (egg sandwich) with meringue-coated yolk, baked to order.
- **Gion Tsujiri Saryo Tsujiri Daimaru Shinsaibashi Store** [Shinsaibashi]: Osaka-limited matcha parfait experience for devoted matcha lovers.
- **Horikan** [Shimmachi] | Tabelog 3.46*: A retro café offering a morning set (sandwich, bacon and eggs, salad, coffee) for just ¥550 in a cozy park-front setting.

## Bars (5)
- **Bible Club** [Namba]: Cocktail bar praised for amazing cocktails.
- **Izakaya Toyo** [Kyobashi]: A favourite izakaya near Kyobashi Station praised for its authentic, gritty atmosphere with less tourist crowds than Dotonbori.
- **Izakaya Tsukidoro**: Lunch-limited 1,000-yen tamagoyaki (rolled omelette) set meal, known for an orange yolk visual.
- **Nonkiya** [Dotonbori]: Standing bar specialising in oden and tendon skewers simmered in miso sauce; praised as a great heritage restaurant.
- **PC and Retro Bar Space Station** [Osaka]: Retro gaming and PC bar in Osaka recommended as a unique night-out option in the city.

## Markets (2)
- **Kuromon Ichiba Market** [Namba]: Known as 'Osaka's Kitchen', this covered market sells fresh seafood, wagyu, and local produce and is the best place to experience Osaka's food culture.
- **Shinsaibashi-suji** [Shinsaibashi]: Japan's longest covered shopping arcade, stretching through central Osaka with hundreds of shops and restaurants.

## Shrines (1)
- **Namba Yasaka Shrine** [Namba]: Unique shrine in Namba featuring a giant lion head building, recommended as a Day 2 sightseeing stop in Osaka.

## Activitys (6)
- **Dotonbori** [Namba]: Neon-lit entertainment and dining strip along the Dotonbori canal, the heart of Osaka's food scene and nightlife.
- **Hirakata Park** [Hirakata]: An old-school local theme park between Osaka and Kyoto off the Keihan Line, described as an underrated gem with almost no lines on weekdays and a nostalgic atmosphere.
- **Kaiyukan** [Osaka Bay]: One of the world's largest aquariums, featuring whale sharks and a massive Pacific Ocean tank, recommended for Day 3 in Osaka.
- **Super Nintendo World**: Fun Nintendo theme park with great immersive decoration; Donkey Kong ride and Mario Kart VR are highlights.
- **Tsutenkaku Tower** [Shinsekai]: Iconic retro tower in Shinsekai with a slider and observation deck, symbol of the historic Shinsekai entertainment district.
- **Umeda Sky Building** [Umeda]: A famous Osaka landmark designed by architect Hiroshi Hara, popular for its floating garden observatory views; featured in multiple trip reports and art posts.

## Hotels (5)
- **HOTEL THE FLAG Shinsaibashi** [Shinsaibashi]: Used as home base for a 13-day solo trip to Japan, located in the Shinsaibashi area.
- **Hearton Hotel Nishi Umeda** [Namba/Umeda]: Mentioned as a hotel base for an 11-day Osaka/Kyoto solo trip at around 9,000 yen per night.
- **Hotel Ataroyo**: A capsule-style hotel in Osaka with a Superior Capsule Room upgrade option offering enough space for a solo traveller.
- **Hotel Royal Classic Osaka**: Rated 8/10 as a base hotel during an Osaka stay on a 2-week Japan food-focused trip.
- **Moxy Hotel Honmachi** [Honmachi]: Described as a favourite hotel with on-site laundromat, 24-hour lounge and bar, two blocks from subway.

## Shops (3)
- **Amato Maeda Namba-Walk** [Namba]: Sweet and chewy mitarashi dango specialist inside the Namba Walk shopping arcade.
- **Hanadako** [Dotonbori]: Widely considered the best takoyaki in Osaka by locals, a must-visit street food stall in the Dotonbori area.
- **Kankando Shinsekai** [Shinsekai]: Baby castella (mini sponge cake) shop in Shinsekai, recommended as a street food stop in the district.
